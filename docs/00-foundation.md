## Core Feeling

> The user is "gently watched."
>
> Not pressure. Not gamification. Just **the presence of someone quietly attentive**.
>
> Like: *"Someone is watching, but trusts you to do well."*

This is the single sentence that every design decision in this repo serves.

---

## Six Design Pillars

| # | Principle | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **No UI is the UI** | The card *is* the time picker. The character *is* the Start button. Card flipping *is* mode switching. There are no traditional buttons. |
| 2 | **Cumulative, not streak** | Total focus *days* — never resets. No daily punishment. "I don't need you to show up every day; I want you to come back when you need to." |
| 3 | **Gentle supervision** | Officer wears military uniform but stands languidly. Watches, doesn't pressure. |
| 4 | **World first** | The home screen is a scene, not a tool. Stats and settings live off-stage. |
| 5 | **Low-interruption immersion** | Minimal sound. Companions pass quietly. No strong alerts. Most behaviors are ambient. |
| 6 | **Contrast as soul** | Military uniform + languid pose. Strict discipline + pink heart on the back of the card. The product lives in this tension. |

## Visual Anchors (Quick Reference)

```
Line / Outline:   #2A2128   (warm black, never pure black)
Highlight:        #F4EFE6   (warm white, never pure white)
Neutral grey:     #7A7480
Plant green:      #7B9268   (the "breathing color" across all scenes)
Officer pink:     #E89BAA   (heart accent, Mouse ear/nose)
```

Pure `#000000` and `#FFFFFF` are reserved exclusively for **pixel text**.
